# Founder Note

Status: Project context

Ordo comes from Keith Williams' work across AI education, software
manufacturing, SaaS architecture, and agentic orchestration.

Keith is a Senior University Lecturer at NJIT and Director of the B.S. in
Enterprise AI. He has spent more than 25 years teaching full time in higher
education and has also worked as a SaaS CTO and framework architect.

The project reflects a simple belief: the next leap in AI is not better prompts
alone. It is better systems of work.

Ordo is an attempt to make those systems concrete: workflows where humans set
intent, AI assists with research and production, and verification turns output
into durable software, knowledge, media, and decision artifacts.

The project is self-funded and community-first. That is intentional. Ordo should
be able to develop slowly, publicly, and with strong opinions about governance,
evidence, and ownership before it tries to behave like a conventional software
business.

Ordo is also a teaching instrument. It is meant to help developers, students,
educators, and AI builders move beyond using AI tools toward designing
responsible, repeatable AI systems of work.

This note is background on the project. It is not an institutional endorsement
by NJIT.
